Penaeus monodon
Giant Tiger Shrimp
The Giant Tiger Shrimp (Penaeus monodon) is a large, fast-growing shrimp species native to Indo-Pacific waters. It is characterized by its dark, striped shell and can grow up to 33 cm (13 inches) in length. This species is highly valued in aquaculture due to its size and rapid growth but is also prone to diseases like White Spot Syndrome. It thrives in brackish and marine environments, making it a key species in shrimp farming, especially in Southeast Asia.
